Personalized Robots: Hardware and Software Strategies to Adapt Robotic Support Workshop @ ICRA

Assistive and Rehabilition robots can enhance the quality of life for the elderly, people with disabilities, and their caregivers. Personalization of the autonomy can tailor support to the unique physical and psychological needs of the person and situation. While the actions of the robot can be modified by continuous monitoring and tuning of control parameters, the personalization of hardware has often been limited by fabrication techniques and cost. Recent advances in manufacturing and robotic mechanism design have removed some of these historic barriers to personalization. In this workshop, we explore the benefits of software- and hardware-based personalization, including perspectives on when to pursue one strategy over another. Panelist and participants will answer questions about practical considerations might lead to a preference, what problems require a particular strategy, and how we pursue both strategies simultaneously.

This workshop tutorial will present different techniques to achieve personalization. This workshop will foster multidisciplinary discussion and friendly debate as well as consolidate perspectives, methodologies, and assessment tools to grow research efforts in human-centered robotics.
